Personal Projects

Here I display some personal projects I've worked on. No rhyme or reason to them, just things I'm interested in.

NFC Embedded Ring

This is a ceramic ring with an opal inlay. Under the opal inlay are two NFC chips. The NFC chips can hold contact information, or any other NDEF formatted payload.

Smart Dryer Conversion

I attached a humidity/temperature sensor to the inside of the Dryer wall. The sensor is housed in a high-temperature 3D printed fixture made out of engineering plastic, so it snaps in the wall of the dryer. Using silicone wires, the sensor is powered and controlled by a wi-fi enabled microcontroller that signals a smart speaker when the laundry is dry enough.

Light Up Mask Circuit

I designed a simple mask circuit that lights up when a person is talking. I used Altium and had the PCB printed. This is inserted into a mask insert, and it looks like the mask has a mouth when a person is talking.

WETA

WETA stands for Weather Email Temperature and Appointment. This raspberry pi based system was build to hang on my wall as a functional decoration. The code is Python Based, accessing the google API for email and calendar purposes. It would tell me the precipiation chance percentage, whether or not there was an email waiting for me, the temperature outside, and whether or not I had an upcoming calendar event. All of the notifications were mapped to RGB lights to show events and notifications based on certain colors.

Laser Cut Graduation Cap

This is my design I created for my graduation cap. I found a map of Boston, and overlayed Boston University over it. Then I put the picture through the outline function in Corel Draw, and outputted the file to a Laser Cutter.

Solar Helmet

This is a Solar Helmet I created when I rode my bike through the streets of Boston. I had a bunch of Solar Leafletts hanging around, so I put them on a acrylic backing and put a protective coating of two-part epoxy. The point was to trickle charge batteries that were powering lights lining the edge of my helmet, as to not get hit by Boston drivers.

Word Qlock

This is a project that I found on Instructables.com. It uses an ATMEGA chip with a crystal oscillator to keep track of the time and display the time in words rather than the traditional digital or analog method.

Vanity Mirror

This vanity Mirror was created as a gift. It was a little too bright whoops.

Wireless Charger

This wireless charger was created as a docking station for my Samsung Galaxy S6. It uses a wireless charging module and some wood.

Mother's Day Sign

Using a plank of wood, I laser cut a heart-felt message as a Mother's Day gift.
