School Projects

On this page I display some of the bigger school projects I've worked on. Most of the projects are from Boston University but I also included my Challenge Project from the Gordon Institute of Engineering Leadership at Northeastern.

Needle Pump Challenge Project

This is a challenge project I worked on for the Gordon Institute of Engineering Leadership. It is a pumping mechanism that operates through the linear displacement of a stainless-steel cannula. It involved ideation, 3D printing, molding, then testing the needle pump parts.

BU Senior Capstone Anheuser-Busch

My team and I developed a design of a Tannic Acid bulk movement system for Anheuser-Busch. A 3D mockup was also developed in the process to demonstrate how the system would function.

Automated Cymbal Hammer

For a class called Electromechanical Design, the final project was to do a preliminary design of a automated cymbal hammer. This system would take blank cymbals, and put dimples into the surface based on the desired surface scatter of said dimples. The purpose of the dimples is to create a higher quality sound for the symbals. This is a picture of the system CAD. If you download it, its a moving GIF

Automated Water Bottler

This is the final report of the Automated Water bottler that our group created for a Product Design class. The water bottler is an arm that takes an empty water bottles and swing them under a sensor to fill said water bottles. This is a subsystem of an overall system whose function would be to create an onsite water bottling station.

908 Technologies Bulk Vaporizer

For a class called Introduction to Engineering Design, my group and I designed a device that would work in conjunction with a portable mass spectrometer that 908 Technologies developed. The issue was that the mass spectrometer could nnot identify bulk materials well, so our group was assigned to create a vaporizer for said bulk materials.

Drone Thrust Sensor

This is a Drone Thrust sensor I developed for research at the robotics laboratory on the Boston University campus. The concept is that the structure would all be on a strain gauge embedded into a Wheatstone bridge. Any thrust that the drone produces would be measured by the strain gauge. This setup was meant for a laboratory exercise that would teach Dynamics students about Newton's 3rd Law using drones.
